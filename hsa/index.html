The Process

HOW AN HSA ACTUALLY WORKS

01
One-time setup

Your corporation becomes the plan sponsor

Frank registers your business under a CRA-compliant Private Health Services Plan (PHSP). This takes a 15-minute call and typically 1–2 business days to activate. You pay nothing. Sole proprietors qualify too — with slightly different rules we'll cover on the call.

02
Ongoing — anytime you have a bill

You pay the medical expense personally, keep the receipt

Dental work done. Glasses picked up. Prescription filled. Pay it out of your personal account like you always have — but now you keep every receipt. Eligible expenses include anything on the CRA medical expense list.

03
Tax-free reimbursement

Your corporation reimburses you, deducts the full amount

Submit your receipts through the plan. Your corporation cuts you a reimbursement cheque — that's not taxable income to you. The corporation deducts 100% of the claim plus the small admin fee as a business expense. That's the whole mechanism.

The Numbers

WHAT IT ACTUALLY SAVES YOU

Here's a concrete example. Say you spend $3,000 this year on dental, glasses, and physio — a realistic number for a family. Without an HSA, you pay that $3,000 from personal after-tax dollars: money that's already been taxed. With an HSA, your corporation pays the claim and deducts the full amount at Alberta's small business rate. The savings are modest on small amounts — but they compound. A family spending $5,000–$8,000 annually on medical expenses sees real money stay in the corporation, and the admin fee is deductible too. The bigger case is this: you're converting a personal expense into a legitimate business deduction. That's the mechanism.
